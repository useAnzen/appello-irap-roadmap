1 · Objective

Cross-team coordination happens over the same appello-roadmap/plans/ + bug-report-agent surfaces; R1 evidence is already captured in DocArtifact (40+ glossary terms) + 30 bug reports. This epic establishes the recurring C1 ↔ C3 sync cadence: weekly 30 min coordination so the SDLC pipeline (C1) and the knowledge/test stack (C3) ship aligned — e.g. C1 ships sdlc-auditor rules that C3 then validates via Spider; C3 surfaces ATLAS retrieval gaps that C1 fixes in decomposer.

2 · Acceptance Criteria

  • Cadence: weekly Tuesday 30 min; recurring meeting.
  • Standing agenda: what-shipped-on-each-side (10 min), inter-team blockers (10 min), upcoming-week alignment (10 min).
  • Inter-team artifacts logged at appello-roadmap/coordination/c1-c3-syncs/YYYY-MM-DD.md.
  • Blocker triage: each blocker tagged with blocking-team, blocked-team, severity, eta-to-unblock.
  • Joint backlog page filterable by cross-team blockers.
  • Quarterly retro: are syncs producing decisions? Cancel if pure status-recital.
  • First 8 syncs minuted + shared in #engineering.

3 · Architecture & Approach

Sync flow

Mon EOD: each team posts "this week's ship + asks" in a shared doc
Tue AM: 30-min sync runs the agenda against that doc
Tue PM: sync minutes published; blockers tagged in DB
Wed-Fri: blockers tracked on joint backlog page

4 · Implementation Phases (20h)

  1. Cadence + calendar (2h).
  2. Agenda template + blocker schema (3h).
  3. Joint backlog page (4h).
  4. First 8 syncs facilitated (8h).
  5. Quarterly retro + adjustment (3h).

5 · Risks & Mitigations

  • Sync becomes status-only. Mitigation: 30 min hard cap; blockers section gets ≥ 50% of time.
  • Blocker tagging not maintained. Mitigation: stale blockers auto-flag after 7d.
  • Calendar conflict with sprint planning. Mitigation: separate day from C1-0037 cadence.
