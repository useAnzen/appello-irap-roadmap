1 · Objective

Validate the proposed scope of the C4 Apex toolbox set — the in-repo Mastra tools shipped under packages/agentc2/src/tools/ and the matching Cursor skills under .cursor/skills/ — against real field-worker pain. Five 45-minute structured interviews with foremen, crew leads, equipment operators, estimators, and bookkeepers across the existing Appello customer base produce a transcribed, coded, and ranked inventory of software pain points. The ranked top-10 anchors the four sibling toolbox epics (C4-0342 TBX-1, C4-0343 FIN-1, C4-0344 TS-1, C4-0345 PS-1) and gates which toolboxes ship in P1 vs. defer to P2.

This is a discovery epic, not an implementation one — the deliverable is evidence, not code. The output (transcripts + ranked report + toolbox-mapping table) becomes the citation backbone for every Apex toolbox UIS authored after April 26.

2 · Acceptance criteria

  1. 5 interviews complete: one per persona (Foreman, Crew Lead, Equipment Operator, Estimator, Bookkeeper / Office Admin), each recorded end-to-end via Fathom and fully transcribed.
  2. ≥ 20 distinct pain points logged in the raw inventory — each tagged with persona, current-software, blast-radius, workaround, and a verbatim quote pointer (timestamp + speaker).
  3. Ranked top-10 report committed at agentc2/docs/apex-discovery/c4-0341-ranked-pains.md using the Severity × Frequency × Apex-fit rubric in §9, with written justification per item.
  4. Toolbox-mapping table in the same doc that assigns every top-10 pain to one of TBX-1 / FIN-1 / TS-1 / PS-1 or to an explicit "no-fit / refer-out" bucket, plus a recommended P1-vs-P2 sequencing.
  5. Disconfirmation logged: at least two pain points that were on the pre-interview hypothesis list but failed to surface across interviews are explicitly retired with a one-line "killed because…" note (we are not just looking for confirmation).
  6. Source traceability: every claim in the ranked report links back to a Fathom transcript URL + timestamp; no quote is paraphrased without the verbatim original alongside it.
  7. Hand-off artifact: a single Notion page (or committed markdown) titled Apex Toolbox Discovery — April 26 that the four sibling epics can cite as source: c4-0341.
  8. NRC defensibility: recruitment script, consent language, and an anonymization pass on the published report are committed alongside the raw transcripts so the discovery activity is auditable as IRAP-eligible R&D effort.

3 · Interview target selection — 5 personas + recruitment

One interview per persona. Each persona is a job role inside an existing Appello customer (sourced from the appello/plan/sales-intel/deals/ brief library); the goal is to span the day-in-the-life roles that touch software end-to-end, not to oversample one segment.

P1 · Foreman on-site supervisor, 5–25 crew

Runs the daily morning huddle, dispatches crews, signs off on daily progress, gets the most "I need this from the office" interruptions. Highest touch-points with scheduling, time-tracking, and material-callout software.

  • Recruit from: Appello Mechanical, Vanos, Rival Insulation, RR Mechanical (deal briefs under appello/plan/sales-intel/deals/).
  • Tenure filter: ≥ 18 months as foreman so they can compare current tooling to the prior workflow.

P2 · Crew Lead working lead-hand, 2–6 trades

Hands-on, but still owns daily tickets, photo documentation, and crew time submission. The person who actually uses the mobile app under load — wet gloves, no signal, time pressure.

  • Recruit from: existing mobile app users active in the last 30 days (cross-reference the customer instance MCPs — Appello-AppelloMechanical, Appello-Vanos, Appello-RivalInsulation).
  • Tenure filter: currently submitting weekly via mobile.

P3 · Equipment Operator heavy-equipment or specialty-trade operator

Lower software touch overall but very specific friction: equipment-hour logging, pre-trip inspections, fuel/maintenance tickets. The persona most likely to abandon a workflow if it takes more than 30 seconds.

  • Recruit from: a customer running equipment-intensive work (DM Industries or AARC West candidates).
  • Tenure filter: ≥ 12 months operating; uses at least one digital pre-trip checklist today (even if it's a paper form scanned in).

P4 · Estimator office-based takeoff & bid prep

Sits between sales and operations. Heavy spreadsheet user; most likely to articulate "why am I retyping this number for the fourth time today." Strong candidate for the TBX-1 / FIN-1 toolbox loop.

  • Recruit from: a customer that bids work (Thomas Kanata, Rival Insulation, Appello Mechanical).
  • Tenure filter: currently producing ≥ 2 estimates / week.

P5 · Bookkeeper / Office Admin accounting, payroll, AR/AP

The accounting-system integration end of every workflow. Knows exactly which fields don't round-trip between Appello and Sage / QuickBooks / Wave. Anchors the FIN-1 toolbox.

  • Recruit from: a customer with an external accounting integration in flight (AARC West / Sage; Prometrix / Wave).
  • Tenure filter: owns weekly payroll or monthly close.

3.1 Recruitment criteria — hard rules

  • One interview per company. No more than one persona drawn from the same customer instance — avoids over-fitting the ranked list to a single account's idiosyncrasies.
  • Active user, not a champion. Recruit the person who uses the software daily, not the executive sponsor who signed the contract. Cross-check via the customer's instance MCP (last-login < 30 days).
  • Mix the accounting systems. At least two of the five customers must run different accounting back-ends (e.g. one Sage, one QuickBooks, one Wave) so the bookkeeper interview surfaces integration pain in plural.
  • $50 gift card honorarium for the field-worker personas (P1–P3) — booked through Hubspot as a "research credit" deal-line so it's auditable for IRAP.
  • No NDAs required; participants sign the lightweight research-consent form in §3.3 covering recording, anonymization, and quote use.

4 · Interview protocol — 45-minute structured script

Run identically across all five interviews so the synthesis is comparable. Four blocks. Twelve verbatim questions. Probes (§5) deployed as needed inside each block.

BlockDurationGoalStyle
A · Warm-up5 minLower interview pressure; map the person's actual job.Open, conversational.
B · Current software landscape10 minInventory every piece of software they touch in a normal week.Listing, no judgement.
C · Workflow walkthrough20 minWalk one real recent task end-to-end; capture friction at every hop.Contextual inquiry — silence is the interviewer's main tool.
D · Wishlist + ranking10 minGet their top 3 pain points in their own words; sanity-check magnitude.Forced-choice; rank in the call.

4.1 Block A — Warm-up (5 min)

Q1Walk me through what you actually do in a typical day. Start at "I get to the truck / I open my laptop" — what's the first thing you touch, and what's the last?Goal: anchor the rest of the interview in their real shift, not their job title.
Q2If I shadowed you for a week, what would I see you doing that surprises most people who don't do your job?Goal: surface the hidden labour — the stuff that doesn't appear on any ticket.

4.2 Block B — Current software landscape (10 min)

Q3List every piece of software, app, or paper form you touch in a normal week. I'll write them down — don't worry about order, just keep going until you've got them all.Goal: inventory. Get the full stack on the page before judging any of it. Expect 6–15 items.
Q4Of everything on that list, which one do you spend the most time in each week? Which one do you most dread opening? Are they the same one?Goal: separate volume-pain from emotional-pain — they often diverge and the divergence is the signal.
Q5What's something you do today on paper, in a text message, or in a phone call — that you'd genuinely rather do in software, if the software didn't suck?Goal: surface latent demand for tools that don't exist yet. The TBX-1 anchor question.

4.3 Block C — Workflow walkthrough (20 min)

Pick one task they did this week. Not last month, not a typical one — a specific concrete one they remember. Walk it minute-by-minute. Use the probes in §5 liberally.

Q6Pick a real task you finished in the last seven days — could be a daily ticket, an estimate, a payroll close, anything. I want to walk through it with you step by step. What was it?Goal: pin down a concrete instance. Block any "well, usually I…" answers.
Q7OK — start at the moment you knew you had to do this task. Where did the information come from? Who told you, or what app pinged you, or what was on the schedule?Goal: trace the trigger. Trigger-pain is often the highest blast-radius pain.
Q8Walk me to the next step. What did you open? What did you type? If you had to copy something from one place to another, what was the source and what was the destination?Goal: catch every copy-paste hop. Each hop is a candidate Apex tool.
Q9Where in this task did you get stuck, frustrated, have to wait for someone, or have to redo something? Walk me through that specific moment — what was on your screen, who did you ping, how long did it take to unblock?Goal: this is the core question. Time-on-screen + blocker-identity = the blast-radius score in §8.
Q10If you had a magic button that could have skipped one step of this task — not the whole thing, just one step — which step would you delete, and what would the magic button do?Goal: forces the user to articulate the highest-leverage automation in their own words. Direct candidate-toolbox input.

4.4 Block D — Wishlist + in-call ranking (10 min)

Q11Forget what's realistic. If I gave you an assistant that could do one thing for you — anything — what would you ask it to do first?Goal: blue-sky framing surfaces ambition that constrained framing misses. Often reveals the PS-1 anchor.
Q12Now back to reality. Rank your top three software frustrations from this conversation. Number one is "if you fixed this tomorrow I'd hug you"; number three is "it bugs me but I've adapted." Take a minute — I'll wait.Goal: forced ranking in the call, while context is hot. Do not skip the silent minute.
Q13Last one — is there anything I didn't ask about that you'd want the people building this stuff to know? Anything you came into this call hoping I'd ask?Goal: cleanup. Catches the framing the protocol missed. Roughly 1 in 5 calls produces the most useful quote here.
Interviewer discipline. Average pain-point density in an unscripted "tell me your problems" call is < 5 items per hour. With this protocol the target is ≥ 6 pain points per interview (30+ across five calls before deduplication). If a call is running thin by the end of Block C, drop directly into the probe bank (§5) — do not volunteer your own pain hypotheses to fill silence.

5 · Probe bank — 10 follow-ups to deploy on demand

Use whenever the participant gives a thin answer ("yeah, it sucks", "the software is slow") or jumps over a hop in the workflow walkthrough. Order does not matter; pick the one that fits the moment.

PR1Show me — can you screen-share that for 30 seconds and walk me through what you just did?
PR2How long did that take you the last time it happened? And how many times this week did you do it?
PR3If that step broke for an entire day, what would the downstream impact be? Who'd be the first to notice?
PR4What did you do before this software existed — how did you handle this in 2018? Was it actually worse, or just differently bad?
PR5Walk me through the workaround. There's clearly a workaround — what is it, and who taught it to you?
PR6When this goes wrong, do you fix it yourself, or do you escalate? To whom?
PR7Is there a coworker who handles this better than you do? What do they do differently?
PR8Have you ever just stopped doing this step? What happened? Did anyone notice?
PR9If I gave you exactly this software but it ran twice as fast, would the pain go away? Or is the problem deeper than speed?
PR10Was there a moment in the last six months where this same problem cost the company real money or a real customer? Tell me about that specific moment.

6 · Synthesis framework — coding rubric per pain point

Every distinct pain point surfaced across the five interviews gets one row in the raw inventory spreadsheet (agentc2/docs/apex-discovery/raw-pain-inventory.csv). The coding rubric is fixed in advance so two coders (Nathan + Corey or Nathan + a Fathom-only spot-check) produce comparable rows.

ColumnTypeRule
pain_idstringMonotonic, e.g. PAIN-001. Stable for cross-doc citation.
personaenumForeman | CrewLead | EquipOp | Estimator | Bookkeeper. Exactly one.
quote_verbatimstringThe exact words the participant used, ≤ 200 chars. No paraphrasing.
fathom_refurlFathom transcript URL with timestamp anchor (e.g. ?t=1247).
current_softwarestringThe named tool the pain is anchored in (e.g. "Sage 100", "WhatsApp", "paper inspection form").
frequencyenumDaily | Weekly | Monthly | Rare. Derived from PR2.
blast_radiusenumSelf | Crew | Office | Customer | Cash. Derived from PR3 and Q9.
workaround_existsboolTrue if a workaround was described in PR5; capture it in workaround_notes.
workaround_notesstringFree text describing the current workaround.
candidate_toolboxenumTBX-1 | FIN-1 | TS-1 | PS-1 | NO-FIT. The first-pass assignment to a sibling epic.
apex_fit_notesstringOne sentence on why this is or is not a fit for the Apex toolbox surface (i.e. could it be a Mastra tool under packages/agentc2/src/tools/?).

6.1 Inter-coder check

After the first two interviews are coded, Nathan and Corey independently code a 10-row sample of the third interview's pain points. If candidate_toolbox agreement is < 80%, the rubric is tightened (typically by writing explicit boundary examples for the TBX-1 vs. PS-1 line) before continuing.

6.2 Deduplication

Pain points are merged when (a) the same current_software + workflow hop is described by ≥ 2 personas, or (b) the verbatim quotes are semantically equivalent under a same-sentence test. Merged rows carry every fathom_ref in a list so frequency is not double-counted but provenance is preserved.

7 · Ranking method — Severity × Frequency × Apex-fit

The raw inventory is deduplicated and scored. Top-10 by composite score becomes the ranked report.

// Composite score per pain point
const score = severity * frequency * apexFit;

// Severity — derived from blast_radius
Severity: {
    "Self": 1, "Crew": 2, "Office": 3,
    "Customer": 4, "Cash": 5
}

// Frequency — direct from coded frequency column
Frequency: {
    "Rare": 1, "Monthly": 2,
    "Weekly": 3, "Daily": 5
}

// Apex-fit — qualitative judgment, written rationale required
//   1 = not a software problem (e.g. "the truck breaks down")
//   2 = software but outside C4 (e.g. "I want darker mode")
//   3 = solvable by an existing AgentC2 tool with config (rag-tools.ts, web-search.ts)
//   4 = solvable by writing one new Mastra tool under packages/agentc2/src/tools/
//   5 = solvable by a new toolbox (a small group of tools + a skill) — the ideal Apex shape
ApexFit: { "NoFit": 1, "Outside": 2, "Config": 3, "OneTool": 4, "Toolbox": 5 }

Max composite = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125. The cutoff for the top-10 is rank, not threshold — even a thin batch must produce 10 ranked items so the toolbox-mapping table is forced to make discriminating calls.

7.1 Justification template per top-10 item

## PAIN-007 · "I send the same 3 photos to the office twice a day" (Foreman)
**Score:** 4 (Customer) × 5 (Daily) × 5 (Toolbox) = **100**
**Mapped to:** TBX-1 — Field Capture toolbox
**Why:** A Mastra tool wrapping `photo-ingest + auto-classify + push-to-job`
  is a 3–5 day build that absorbs the entire workflow hop. Touches
  `packages/agentc2/src/tools/document-tools.ts` for storage and a new
  classifier tool. Skill at `.cursor/skills/appello-field-capture/`.
**Disconfirming evidence:** Bookkeeper (P5) reported zero touch with photos
  → confirms this is a field-only pain, not company-wide.

7.2 Ties and ordering inside the top-10

Ties broken by (1) number of personas that surfaced the pain (breadth wins), (2) presence of an active workaround (existing workarounds ⇒ higher cost-of-status-quo), (3) interviewer judgement, logged.

Sequencing rule. A toolbox ships in P1 only if it absorbs ≥ 3 distinct top-10 pains and at least one pain is rated Severity ≥ 4 (Customer or Cash). Toolboxes that absorb < 3 top-10 pains slide to P2 — and that defer-decision is itself a deliverable of this epic, not a separate planning meeting.

11 · Risks & mitigations

RiskImpactMitigation
Recruitment delay — field workers do not check email; foremen are hard to reach mid-week; the gift card alone isn't always enough. High on timeline Recruit through the customer's executive sponsor as a warm intro (one Hubspot deal-owner ping per interview). Have eight prospects in flight to land five interviews. Start outreach in week 1 even if other prep is incomplete.
Interviewer bias — Nathan knows the Apex toolbox roadmap and may unconsciously lead participants toward pain points that fit the existing hypothesis. High on validity Run the verbatim script. Use silence as a tool, not your own hypothesis. After interview #2, ask Corey to spot-check one transcript for leading questions. Pre-register the disconfirmation hypotheses (acceptance criterion #5) so killed-hypotheses are tracked, not silently dropped.
Small N = 5 — five interviews cannot prove a pain point is universal; one participant's quirk can dominate the ranked report. Med The forced one-per-persona quota (§3) plus the breadth-tiebreak rule in §7.2 prevents a single participant's pain from owning more than one top-10 slot. The ranked report explicitly captions itself as "directional, N=5" and the sibling epics must cite ≥ 1 corroborating customer signal (deal brief, support ticket, ATLAS retrieval) before sizing a toolbox build.
Persona drift — the foreman recruited is actually a working owner, or the bookkeeper is a generalist office admin; "Foreman" data is mis-tagged. Med Day-in-the-life answer to Q1 acts as the persona check. If the answer doesn't match the persona definition in §3, the call is re-tagged in the inventory and a make-up interview is booked.
Customer-conflict pain — a participant raises a pain that is also an open support ticket; the "research" call becomes a service call. Low Capture the ticket, hand it to support after the call, return the conversation to the script. Do not commit to a fix in the call — it skews the wishlist questions.
Audio quality on jobsite — foreman / crew lead calls taken from a truck or site have wind, machinery, and dropouts. Low Default to a phone call (Fathom records via dial-in) rather than video for P1–P3. Schedule the call window during the participant's commute or lunch break, not on-site.
Consent / anonymization slip — a verbatim quote in the published report names a coworker or competitor. Med for trust Anonymization pass before the report leaves the Apex Discovery folder. Strip names, replace company names with role + region (e.g. "a Western Canadian insulation contractor"). Audit packet in agentc2/docs/apex-discovery/recruitment/ retains the originals.
