1 · Objective

appello-roadmap/plans/ holds UIS specs; agentc2/packages/agentc2/src/mcp/api-clients/jira.ts is the Jira integration. Once NH1/NH3 onboard the team is large enough to run real sprint ceremonies. This epic establishes the recurring sprint-planning cadence that takes from plans/ (groomed by C1-0017) and turns into prioritised work_items rows ready for autonomous dispatch — bridging the solo-grooming era into the team era.

2 · Acceptance Criteria

  • Sprint cadence: 2-week sprints starting Mon; planning Mon AM (90 min); retro Fri PM (45 min).
  • Planning input: top of plans/SLATE-4WEEK.md + any urgent Jira tickets via jira.ts.
  • Planning output: ≥ 12 work-items committed per sprint; each tied to a hamburger slug + estimated days; assigned to a person OR agent:cursor-coding-agent.
  • Capacity model: Christopher 6 sprint-days, Ian 6, NH1 4 (ramp), NH3 4 (ramp), autonomous-factory 10 (eq.).
  • Sprint board visible at /pipeline/sprints/current; closeout writes velocity to SprintMetrics.
  • Retro template: what-worked / what-broke / one-experiment-next.
  • Cadence persistence: planning agenda template + meeting notes in appello-roadmap/sprints/.

4 · Implementation Phases (20h)

  1. Cadence proposal + Corey sign-off (3h).
  2. Capacity model + first-sprint commitments (4h).
  3. Sprint board page + Sprint model + WorkItem.sprintId (6h).
  4. Jira import sub-flow via jira.ts (4h).
  5. Retro template + velocity capture (3h).

5 · Risks & Mitigations

  • NH ramp slower than 4 sprint-days. Mitigation: capacity revised at sprint 2 retro; plan adjusted.
  • Autonomous factory throughput overestimated. Mitigation: velocity is measured, not assumed; capacity-model auto-updates from prior 3 sprints.
  • Sprint commitments break with mid-sprint urgent Jira. Mitigation: 20% slack budget per sprint; explicit swap rule.
