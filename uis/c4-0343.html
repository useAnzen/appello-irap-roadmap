1 · Objective

Same customer-pilot surfaces as C4-0341: Fathom MCP + appello/plan/sales-intel/deals/ + Hubspot MCP. This epic is the Ian-led customer-interview series specifically focused on Apex buyers + champions (vs C4-0341's field-worker focus): 8 interviews with decision-makers (project managers, ops directors, finance) to validate that the field-worker pain points have buyer-side resonance + ROI sponsorship — without this validation, even the best-built Apex skills don't get bought.

2 · Acceptance Criteria

  • 8 buyer-side interviews completed within 8 weeks across ≥ 5 customers.
  • Interview protocol at appello/plan/apex-research/buyer-protocol.md: 30 min, focused on willingness-to-pay + workflow fit + change-management appetite.
  • Each interview captured in Fathom; tagged apex-buyer:<date>:<customer>.
  • Per-interview synthesis covers: which field-pain-points the buyer recognises, what they'd pay per seat per month, what would block adoption.
  • Cross-referenced with C4-0341 field findings to surface buyer↔worker alignment (or misalignment) per pain point.
  • Ranked Apex roadmap at appello/plan/apex-research/APEX-ROADMAP-RANKED.md: per pain-point rows with worker-impact, buyer-WTP, technical-feasibility, recommendation.
  • Roadmap presented to Corey + Ian + Christopher for sign-off; top-5 become C4-0344+ build targets.
  • Hubspot deals updated with each buyer's apex-interest tag for downstream sales motion.

5 · Risks & Mitigations

  • Buyers say everything is critical. Mitigation: forced-choice questions ("if you could only buy ONE..."); WTP elicitation explicit.
  • Buyer/worker mismatch on top items. Mitigation: mismatch explicitly surfaced; product strategy decides whether to fix the buyer narrative or pivot the build.
  • Sales-cycle pressure to commit pre-build. Mitigation: roadmap is internal; no firm commits until sign-off.
