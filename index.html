How Appello builds in an AI-powered world

Every unit of work is captured as a UIS — Unified Implementation Spec: a single-page document covering objective, acceptance criteria, architecture, implementation phases, risks, evidence (linked to real files in real repositories), and cross-references to neighbouring work. The UIS is what the human author, the autonomous SDLC pipeline, and the reviewer all read from. It is the contract.

We plan in detail on a rolling ~30-day horizon. That is why this roadmap shows depth at the front and breadth at the back:

  • DONE — work completed in Apr/May 26. Each has a UIS describing what was built and pointing to the merged code.
  • WORK IN PROCESS — Apr–May 26 in-flight. Each has a UIS authored before work began.
  • PLANNED NEXT — Jun 26 ready-to-build. UIS specs are authored ~30 days ahead, so the June batch already has full specs visible here.
  • TO DO — Jul 26 onward. Visible as forward-roadmap cards (title, owner, month, hours). Their UIS specs are authored in the month before work begins, following the same 30-day cadence.

Click any active epic card below to open its UIS. The 78 active epics (DONE / WIP / PLANNED NEXT) all have full UIS specs live in this site. The 465 forward TO DO epics are the same epics tracked in the Master Workbook — they will gain UIS pages on rolling 30-day windows as the project moves forward.
